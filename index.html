Legal Advocacy
Immigration Law Advocacy

I provide per diem as well as longer-term assistance to law firms and immigration organizations, representing clients before the U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS), Immigration Court (IJ), Bureau of Immigration Appeals (BIA) and the U.S. Department of Justice.


Over the course of my career, my passion has been the pro bono work I've done, representing immigrants in various “fear of return” proceedings before the USCIS and IJs. I won my first Asylum case in 1996 and I’m thrilled to be able to devote more of my time to this area of the law. I have represented immigrants in a variety of matters including:

  • Asylum applications and hearings before the USCIS Asylum Hearing Office
  • Asylum proceedings in Immigration Court
  • Adjustment of Status (Green Card) applications and interviews
  • Naturalization (Citizenship) applications and interviews
  • TPS applications
  • SIJS applications
  • Motions to reopen proceedings
  • Motions for change of venue
  • Bond redetermination hearings
  • Withholding ofremoval and CAT applications
  • Reasonable Fear Interviews
  • Cancellation of Removal merits hearings

About Me

I began my career as an associate attorney in the litigation department of a mid-sized regional law firm. After several years there, I moved on to a series of increasingly greater leadership roles at a captive commercial litigation operation which eventually became known as Siegal & Park. Over the decades there, I mediated, arbitrated, and tried a variety of cases. For part of my career, I also worked as an in-house litigation attorney, overseeing complex coverage matters as a Vice President of a large, multinational insurance company.


I am licensed to practice before the courts in the states of New York and New Jersey and various Federal District and Circuit Courts. I am also registered with the Executive Office of Immigration Review to practice before the Immigration Courts and the Board of Immigration Appeals.
